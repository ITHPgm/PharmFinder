Location Troubleshooting
1
Check your browser permissions Go to your browser settings → Privacy → Location, and ensure PharmFinder is allowed.
2
Enable device GPS Make sure your phone's location services are turned on in system settings.
3
Try a manual search If GPS isn't available, you can always type your area or city in the search bar.
4
Refresh and try again Close and reopen the app, or tap the refresh button on the map.

Frequently Asked Questions
Why can't I see nearby pharmacies?
First, check that your location permission is enabled in your browser settings. If you're in a rural area, there may be fewer pharmacies listed. Try expanding your search radius or searching by city name instead.
Why is a pharmacy marked as closed when it's actually open?
Pharmacy hours rely on a combination of submitted data and community reports. If you find incorrect information, please use the "Report Status" feature on the pharmacy's detail page to help us update it for everyone.
Do I need to create an account to use PharmFinder?
No! PharmFinder works without any sign‑up or login. You can search, view, and get directions instantly. An account is only needed if you want to list your own pharmacy or save favorites across devices.
How do I list my pharmacy on PharmFinder?
Visit the "List Your Pharmacy" section on the homepage, or create an account and navigate to the pharmacy listing form. It's free to list your basic information, with optional premium features available.
Is my location data stored or shared?
No. Your location is used only in real-time to find nearby pharmacies and is never stored, tracked, or shared with any third party. Your privacy is a core commitment of PharmFinder.
Important Notices
Information Accuracy: Pharmacy hours, availability, and stock information may not always be 100% accurate. We rely on a combination of submitted data and community reports. Always verify before visiting, especially for urgent medication needs.

Medical Disclaimer: PharmFinder is a location tool — it does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional for medical concerns.
